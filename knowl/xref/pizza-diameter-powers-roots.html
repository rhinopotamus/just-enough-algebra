The size of a round pizza is described by its diameter. It turns out that we can calculate how many people are served by a pizza of diameter \(D\) inches as \(0.015625 \times D \wedge 2=\text{.}\) For example, a 16-inch diameter pizza serves \(0.015625 \times 16 \wedge 2 = 4\) people. (The mysterious number 0.015625 comes from a little geometry and pizza science.)
